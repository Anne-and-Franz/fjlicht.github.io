About

I have studied a mix of biology, ecology, computational biology, and plant pathology.
 
	      
	      An alumnus of Pennsylvania State University, I have worked in numerous research fields, from mycorrhizal fungi with Dr. Roger Koide, to human pathology at the Walter Reed Army Institute of Research.

	      
	      
	      I now work on agricultural plant pathogens, particularily fungal pathogens of potato.
 
	      
	      
	      I began my graduate studies at the University of New Hampshire in Plant Biology, before moving with the Broders lab to CSU.
 
	      
	      
	      I have traveled to Senegal in Western Africa for a National Science Foundation fellowship to study soil microbial ecology.
 And now live in Fort Collins, Colorado.
 
	      
	      
	      With my personal time I get outside, work on my current research interests and recreational activities.
